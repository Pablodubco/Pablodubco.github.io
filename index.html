A B O U T

So… there we were, just three average coworkers in panic, staring at a screen in utter despair. Almost half a year's worth of processed data defiled by a sadistic query that'd been given the wrong parameters, and we had to recover it by the end of the week. I was the “Excel guy” (every office's got one), and that's it... there really isn’t much more to the story, no evil plot to conquer the mortgage credit business, no romance, no ultra hacking typing on two keyboards at the same time. We pulled an all nighter and were done by the following day. A couple of things stuck with me.

First, coffee will kill me, and I’ll die a happy man. Secondly, that through careful planning, by relying on each other’s strengths, and just by using a series of simple scripts, three persons were able to redo hundredths of hours of work in a single day. That's how powerful coding can be.

I’ve since studied code on my own, learned to use SQL, S-plus, a little bit of java and C#, and, of course, honed even more my Excel-fu. Lately, I've taken up to learning web development. I’m Pablo Dubcovsky, an economist that enjoys min-maxing everything, playing and breaking videogames, and murdering a couple of innocent Android phones just to see how they worked. This basic portfolio, as small and simple as it is, has proven to be a lot of fun, a good challenge, and an important step to acquire the skills needed to dwell in web development. Hope you enjoy it!
